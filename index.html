Smart Buckets

“Smart buckets” is an interactive system based on a set of smart containers, which are capable of recognizing the presence and the type of multiple objects inserted. Each bucket is equipped with a set of led strips that can be used as feedback. “Smart buckets” is also connected to the “Smart room” ecosystem, which enables us to build engaging activities with a focus on object categorization.

The target group of the system is composed of children between 6 and 10 years old with various NDD severity rate (none to moderate). The primary users are not only children but also their therapists and teachers. For this reason we decided to develop: the objects and the activity for children, and an application for tablet as control pannel for teachers and therapists.

The design of the product has been conceived having in mind children and therapists needs, the main aspects that characterize it are listed below:

1. Ergonomics: The container height is the right compromise between capacity and ease of use by children. It is equipped with a battery, hence you are free to place it wherever you wish.

2. Accessibility: The front panel of the bucket can be removed to give access to all the users in a wheelchair.

3. Engagement: To make the product engaging and responsive we decided to put some color leds on the edges of the bucket. This ensures clear feedbacks and children’s engagement.

4. Safety: The shape of the bucket is rounded to ensure children’s safety. Also, all the electronic is hidden to the children and accessible only by the therapist.

5. Extensibility: Using a scale system the therapist is able to rapidly add an object to the system, without worrying to attach any tag to identify the object.

6. Reliability: The system is reliable to ensure a smooth experience for both children and therapists.

Trenord - Website Redesign

The main aim of Trenord is to offer an adequate answer to the raising mobility demand of Lombardy citizen. All the train information are given by website and mobile application, this one was recenty redesigned to improve the usability. The website, on the other hand, have to be redesigned.
We analyzed the website and the application. We conducted a research and analysis and then we compare the main tasks that each persona does on the Trenord website. We uderstood that the most used services are: buy ticket, check train status and check train schedule.
After that, we analyzed in detail each step of each task, the did a schema of the page architecture, we couted the clicks and the number of pages.We analized the website and the application of 3 competitors: Trenitalia, Italo and FSS. We discover some useful insights and then we defindes some parameters. We analized each website and mobile application based on our parameters and we defined some schemes.
After the reseach and analysis phase we started the redesign. First of all we designed a new architecture to solve all the problems that we encounter during the analysis of Trenord website, then we started producing paper mockups. Based on them we defines the website wireframes and then we finalized the style (fonts, colors palette, shapes...)
Finally we developed all the pages of our tasks using Adobe XD.

Cyclot - Park. Play. Care.

Cyclot is an interactive parking lot designed for IGPDecaux. We did researches and analysis about spaces that could used to provide a service, enhance the quality of life in Milan and give advertisements. After the research we decided to go for the Moving around the city scenario, and in particular about cyclists because we saw many problems to solve and was the most fitting one with the IGPDecaux mission. In the concept fase we designed the new asset and different interaction to link the parking lot to the advertisement. At the end we opted for a narrative game based on the quantity of parked bikes. The number of bike can also see as a progressive countdown to relate to a some sort of storytelling that reveal a small part of a bigger story every time.
We want to create a narrative game that is moving forward depending on the amount of the parked bike

1. Gives the feedback when the bike is parked
2. Counts the number of the bikes and moves the narration forwards
3. To win the game residents have to park a certain amount of bikes
4. At the end of the day the game is finishing and the results are showed

We created a 1:1 working prototype and a 10:1 ahestetic prototype. We tested the working prototype during the Demo madness day and we collected some feedback and comments. Folliwing the feedbacks, we decide to work more on the interaction between the user and the asset. We decide to add gesture control to allow user to interact with the whole story. In this way each people parking the bike can interact and see the whole story and see the winning part at the end. We decided to use the light more: at the beginning the light turns on only when the user parks the bike. Now we will use the light every time the user interact with the animation on the screen and when the user win the game the lights do a big amination. We goes from a collective one day long game to a one minute session game where everyone can join at the same time.When nothing is happening we show the company advertisement.

Discover Lotte - An immersive Exhibition

Charlotte "Lotte" Reiniger (2 June 1899 – 19 June 1981) was a German film director and the foremost pioneer of silhouette animation. Her best known films are The Adventures of Prince Achmed, from 1926—thought to be one of the oldest surviving feature-length animated films—and Papageno (1935). Reiniger is also noted for having devised the first form of a multiplane camera; she made more than 40 films, all using her invention.

We decided to structure the exhibition in 4 parts: the introduction to Lotte, the shadow theatre, the immersive space and the interactive DIY part at the end. The introduction to Lotte consist on a curved wall on which we project the introduction video about the subject of our exhibition. The video explains graphically her invention and her film technique.

The next part is a shadow theatre: a 1:1 scale of her layers technique to better explain to visitors her concept. The visitors can download a mobile application and can record a video of them acting in that place.

The following part is the immersive space with some pillow in the center and a big screen projection on the ceiling. Visitors can be immerse in her films and stories. In addiction the walls are animated and shows some information when the user is near. There are also other animations that follow the visitors on the walls like birds, flowers and leafs.

In the last part the user can use her technics in first person thanks to a tablet application and the digitalize version of her multilayer camera.

Trust in Progress - board game

In this 2 weeks workshop we had to develop a board game based on our definition of trust. For us trust is:the resulting process of a personal connection, in which you delegate something or someone based on hope, personal and cultural values and experiences. We asked ourselves some question like:
How do I learn to trust?
How do I decide when to trust?
How do people react when they’re betrayed?

Then we started the ideation phase, we selected some ideas and we composed our proposal:
We are making a physical game for teenagers who will interact with it at home because we want them to define their own set of rules for trusting others in order to know themselves better through failures. We created the board game and we did two days of testing. The final board game bring users to have fun through a decision-making process, face different type of situations and identify themselves in them.

You are suppose to choose between two answers and then accept the consequences. If you picked the good one, you will gain 1 point. If you fail, you will explain to the other players how you will face the new situation. You will discuss with them about your explaination and then they will evaluate your answer giving you from 1 to 3 point. Each point let you to place a pin on your board in the indicated space. At the end of the game you will have a radar schema that describe your personality.

Myni - Follow your emotions

We focused about the relationship between the new generations (x and y) and small expenses. We did researches about users and their relation with money in two forms: cash and card. We use different tools: interviews, observations, questionnaires, and then we sum up our findings in customer journey map and 3 personas. We understood that the trigger of the expenses is not the means but is the amount. In one month the small expenses became really big and no one remember the experience linked to that purchase because it was not important.

So we decided to give value to the little purchases and link them to the user emotion. The system will know the feeling of the user thanks to different sersors and data gathering. Knowing the buying habits and the emotions, the system can suggest which expenses were valuable and which one can maybe be avoided. In this way we will increase the quality and the serenity of users lifes.

The system is composed by a social media campaign, a landing page, a smart bracelet to track the emotions and an application. The bracelet allow users to have a real time feedbacks about the purchase to make him/her more conscious about the expenses. In every moment the user can see the daily amount of purchase and the real time emotion. The feedback is given by coloured led.

Movo - Time management device

How many times did you find yourself in a hurry?

We think that the classic way to measure time are not efficient enough when you are focused on doing something else and so, you lose the sense of time. Movo provides you a new way to organize your schedule in order to be more productive and efficient.

Differenty from a phone alarm clock is not possible to delay or switch the reminders off, movo itself is your visual reminder that keeps working telling you how much time is passing by and you need to get ready.

Movo is mainly composed by two parts: the app and the physical object.

With the app you can set 3 kind of reminders for each event that will be shown by the physical object as a visual and sound feedback.

The green light tells you that you have a task to accomplish that day, also the name of the task will be shown by a notification on your mobile. The red light means that you have to be quick and when the buzzer starts ringing you have no time anymore. You can push it and movo will start swinging and it turns off.

SmartFoody Management system

I really want to deal with the problem of food. I became aware of the seriousness of the problem through desk and field research, interviewing supermarket workers and families in their homes. I also tested people's awareness of this issue with a questionnaire.

From questionnaires to consumers and interviews with managers of several supermarkets show that the most difficult products to monitor are the products stored in the refrigerator departments or in domestic refrigerators. According to the data collected by the WRAP of 2009 the foods most wasted by consumers are fresh ones.

I concentrated on the domestic waste of food, I did a lot of research on possible technologies and finally I decided to use RFID antennas and passive tags.
The company that produces the food products will apply an RFID label on the packaging of the individual product. In supermarkets RFID readers will be placed in the fridge departments. Consumers will have RFID readers inside the refrigerators to monitor food with a single expiry date. Foods that, when opened, must be consumed in a few days, must be monitored in a different way. An RFID HF proximity reader is applied on the front door of the refrigerator, so the system recognizes the food and changes its expiry date considering it "open". Both the reader and the mini one connect to an application via bluetooth.

The App allows consumers to view all the products in the refrigerator in order of expiry, to add leftovers in the system to the system, to generate a shopping list based on the tastes of the family members and to view recipes with ingredients present in the own fridge about to expire.

Arrital showroom experience

Arrital has been designing, manufacturing and selling kitchens for over forty years. The company has noticed that customers are changing and wants to keep up with the times. The brief assigned to us foresees the design of a kitchen suitable for future new customers: the Millennials.

They are digital natives, spend a lot of time on Social Networks and buy through the internet. They seek spaces for socialization because for them, any sort of interaction is essential. They do not buy goods, because they prefer to rent them. Millennials are always looking for interesting experiences to share and want to spend less to have more. Based on the needs of the company and the characteristics of the Millennials, we decided to create an experience around the kitchen.

The location is the Arrital showroom, since less people nowdays went into physical showroom. The experience is based on cooking together following tutorials on the Digital Wall. The kitchen is made up of 5 parts each specialized for a specific task. Thanks to a proximity sensor, the part of the kitchen that is actually used is switched on. The different steps of the tutorial are explained near the location where the activities has to be done by partecipants. The screens light up in sequence according to the needed steps. If the user is still taking a step, the video awaits him. Only after the user has finished, the video will appear at the following location.

We designed 3 different experiences: A small one for 2 or 4 people where partecipants can challenge themselves following the same recipe. A medium one up to 10 people, where friends can cooperate or challenge themselves, eat on a big table and then play some board games. The last one is designed for teambuilding with 4 kitchen modules, a big table and a screen projector for conferences.

Curvalismo - Generative Design

I was inspired by the artistic movements of Divisionism and post Impressionism, to which I added a touch of pop art. I was fascinated by the different painting techniques, in particular the technique used by Vang Gogh and that used by Seurat. I start from a black and white and high contrast photo. The image does not appear on the sketch, but there are lines that recreate the photo below based on its light and dark parts. The lines change their appearance based on the color of the underlying pixel.

If the Pixel is DARK, then the line has: high thickness, low speed, greater curvature.

If the Pixel is CLEAR, then the line has: low thickness, high speed and small curvature.

Initially I created a simple ball that bounces through the Dot class having two methods and two attributes. Then I carried out a color test by filling the ball with the color of the underlying pixel. I created a Dot Array and then, through a linear map, I set the thickness and speed. I set the degree of curvature of the line inversely proportional to the color of the pixel. Finally I thought of a solution for the recognition of the edges for my project. The thickness is no longer linear, but follows the equation of a parabola to be more precise and therefore optimize the program and make the display clearer.

Drop - new way of reading time

Visiting the Salone del Mobile satellite show I was inspired by the watches presented. Most were classic wall or desk clocks with some peculiarity of shape. Very few have tried to do something innovative. I was fascinated by the idea of finding an alternative way to mark, mark and therefore read the time. There are several watches that try to unhinge the archetype. This action culminates in a watch without numbers and hands (fundamental elements that characterize the watch).

The most valid substitutes for numbers and hands are LEDs. The principle of merging LED technology with the classic round shape of the watch was a very valid solution. I thought of a system consisting of 36 LEDs for the hours (3 LEDs for each hour) and 60 LEDs for the minutes. The LEDs were controlled by Arduino through shift registers. I added a form that kept the time and communicated it to Arduino.

After a long work of experimentation of technology emerged always some problem that could not be solved. Initially, it was decided to continue building the prototype by implementing the technological part in the physical model. Later we discovered how to optimize the system by changing the electronic components and greatly reducing the space dedicated to this part. So the need for a change of size and shape arose and in this way Drop was born
